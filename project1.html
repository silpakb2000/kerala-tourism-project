Welcome to God's Own Country

About Kerala Tourism

Kerala, a picturesque state in southwestern India, is celebrated as "God's Own Country" for its breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Its tourism industry thrives on a blend of natural beauty, vibrant traditions, and wellness offerings. From tranquil backwaters and pristine beaches to lush hill stations and vibrant festivals, Kerala offers a diverse array of experiences. Visitors can cruise the serene backwaters on traditional houseboats, relax on stunning beaches, explore misty hill stations, and indulge in traditional Ayurvedic treatments. The state's cultural treasures encompass classical dance forms, art, music, and delectable cuisine. Kerala's wildlife sanctuaries and historical sites add to its allure, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a harmonious blend of nature and culture.

  1. Kottayam

    Giving travelers an opportunity to travel back in time, Kottayam is one of the most serene places to see in Kerala. The ancient town of Kottayam boasts about being rich in culture. With its population achieving hundred percent literacy rate, this town was given the name ‘Akshara Nagari’, that also translates to the City of Letters. Along with being home to several publishing houses, this destination is also the commercial hub of spices and crops. The picturesque views and the ancient shrines simply adds to the beauty of this destination.
